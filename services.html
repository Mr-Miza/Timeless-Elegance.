Fixing Procedures

Initial Inspection:

Upon receiving a Seiko watch for repair, the watchmaker conducts a thorough inspection. They assess the external condition, noting any visible damage, scratches, or signs of wear. This inspection provides insights into potential issues with the watch's components.

Dismantling the Watch:

The watchmaker carefully removes the case back, gaining access to the movement. In the case of automatic movements, they may also disassemble the rotor and oscillating weight. Each component is delicately handled to prevent any damage during the dismantling process.

Cleaning and Lubrication:

Professional watchmakers understand the importance of cleanliness in the intricate world of watch movements. The components are meticulously cleaned using specialized solutions to remove any dirt, dust, or old lubricants. After cleaning, the watchmaker applies precise amounts of high-quality lubricants to critical points within the movement to ensure smooth operation.

Replacement of Worn Components:

During the inspection, if the watchmaker identifies worn-out or damaged parts, these are replaced with genuine Seiko components. This may include gears, springs, or other intricate pieces that contribute to the watch's functionality.

Regulation and Adjustment:

Automatic movements are regulated for accuracy. The watchmaker may use timing machines to measure the watch's performance and adjust the balance wheel, escapement, and hairspring to achieve optimal timekeeping. This process demands a keen understanding of the specific movement type and its intricacies.

Water Resistance Testing:

For watches with water resistance features, the watchmaker ensures that the gaskets and seals are in optimal condition. After any necessary replacements, the watch undergoes water resistance testing to guarantee it meets the specified standards.

Case and Bracelet Restoration:

If the watch case or bracelet requires restoration, the watchmaker may employ polishing and refinishing techniques to bring back the original luster. This enhances the overall aesthetics of the watch.

Quality Control and Final Inspection:

Before returning the watch to the customer, the watchmaker performs a final inspection to ensure all components are functioning correctly. This includes verifying the accuracy of timekeeping, checking the power reserve, and ensuring any additional features (such as date complications) are in proper working order.

Presentation to the Customer:

Once the Seiko watch has undergone comprehensive repairs and quality checks, it is presented to the customer in pristine condition. The watchmaker may also provide recommendations for ongoing maintenance to preserve the watch's longevity.
